Power Grid

In Power Grid, players represent power companies aiming to be the most profitable and efficient by supplying electricity to the most cities. The player who powers the most cities at the end of the game wins.

Objective

To be the most profitable and efficient power company by supplying electricity to the most cities.

Gameplay Overview (Phases of a Round)

Each round consists of five phases:

  1. Determine Player Order: Players are reordered based on the number of cities they have connected (most cities go first).
  2. Auction Power Plants: In reverse player order, players can bid on power plants.
  3. Buy Resources: In reverse player order, players buy resources (coal, oil, garbage, uranium) needed to power their plants.
  4. Build: In player order, players can expand their network by building in cities.
  5. Bureaucracy:
    • Players earn money based on the number of cities they supply with electricity.
    • The resource market is refilled.
    • The power plant market is updated.

Game Steps

The game progresses through three steps, which change certain rules as more cities are built.

  • Step 1: Initial phase.
  • Step 2: Triggered when a player builds a certain number of cities.
  • Step 3: Triggered when the "Step 3" card is drawn.

Winning the Game

The game ends when a player connects a set number of cities. The player who can supply electricity to the most cities wins. In case of a tie, the player with the most money wins.
